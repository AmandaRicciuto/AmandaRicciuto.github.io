About

I am a pediatric gastroenterologist and clinician-scientist at The Hospital for Sick Children (SickKids) in Toronto. My clinical practice and research focus on inflammatory bowel disease (IBD) and primary sclerosing cholangitis (PSC) in children and adolescents. I am particularly interested in understanding the unique phenotype of PSC-IBD, optimizing therapeutic strategies through drug monitoring and treat-to-target approaches, and developing novel therapies for children living with these conditions.

Research

My research program bridges clinical epidemiology, therapeutic optimization, and translational science in pediatric IBD and PSC. Key areas of investigation include:

  • PSC-IBD phenotyping, natural history, and novel therapies including oral vancomycin
  • Gut microbiome alterations in primary sclerosing cholangitis
  • Pharmacogenetics in pediatric IBD (TPMT/NUDT15) and precision dosing
  • Therapeutic drug monitoring and treat-to-target strategies in IBD
  • Colorectal neoplasia surveillance in PSC-IBD
  • Epidemiology and long-term outcomes of pediatric-onset IBD
Consortia & Networks: Paediatric PSC Consortium · Canadian Children IBD Network (CIDsCaNN) · International Organization for the Study of IBD (IOIBD) · Canadian Network for the Study of GI Inflammation in Childhood and the Environment (CANGIEC)
