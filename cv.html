C. Academic Profile
1. Research Statements
Primary Sclerosing Cholangitis in IBD

As an IBD-ologist by training and clinical practice, I bring a unique gut-centric lens to the study of primary sclerosing cholangitis (PSC), a rare but serious liver disease that is intimately linked to IBD. My approach spans multiple research methodologies. During my PhD in Clinical Epidemiology, I compiled a 20-year SickKids PSC/PSC-IBD database and applied longitudinal data analysis to define the gut phenotype in children with PSC, demonstrating frequent subclinical gut inflammation that may contribute to elevated colorectal dysplasia risk. I have since expanded into health services research, securing a Crohn's and Colitis Canada grant to establish a multi-province PSC-IBD cohort linked to health administrative data. To interrogate the biology underlying PSC-IBD, I have built a close and productive collaboration with Dr. Sonya MacParland (basic science immunologist), with whom I co-lead a CIHR Team Grant on autoimmune liver disease and a $1.6M USD Chan Zuckerberg Initiative patient-partnered grant to decipher the cellular ecosystem of PSC at single-cell resolution. Most recently, I was awarded a CIHR Project Grant as PI ($822K, 2025–2029) to unravel the immunological landscape of the PSC gut compared to the non-PSC gut using single-cell approaches. I am deeply involved in major international consortia, including the Pediatric PSC Consortium — through which I led a study demonstrating improved IBD outcomes with oral vancomycin in PSC-IBD — and the NIH-funded ChiLDReN Prospective PSC Study. My international standing in this field is reflected in my invitation to co-author the ESPGHAN Position Paper on PSC in Children with IBD (published in JPGN, 2025) and to lead the PSC chapter of an upcoming NASPGHAN Position Statement on Hepatobiliary Disease and Complications in IBD. I am also deeply committed to patient partnership: through our CZI grant I participate in formal patient education sessions 2–3 times per year, connecting families with PSC to the latest research developments.

Precision Medicine in IBD

A second major pillar of my research program is precision medicine in IBD, encompassing multi-omics biomarker discovery, genomics, and optimized therapeutic dosing. I am PI of the BioMARK-IBD study, a prospective multi-centre Canadian study examining proteomic and transcriptomic predictors of infliximab response in paediatric IBD, leveraging my expertise in longitudinal data analysis to model treatment trajectories. To support this and related translational work, I established the SickKids GI-HEP Biobank, which has enrolled over 200 subjects with a focus on IBD and PSC-IBD. On the genomics front, I am a co-investigator on the Genome Canada-funded PCHSeq initiative ($15.4M; PI: Stephen Scherer), contributing IBD patients from the Biobank to advance precision child health through comprehensive genome sequencing. I also pursue work in pharmacogenomics, contributing to studies on TPMT/NUDT15 variation and the effect of HLA-DQA1*05 genotype on anti-TNF drug levels in paediatric IBD. In the domain of optimized dosing, my group has demonstrated the superiority of body surface area-based infliximab dosing in very early onset IBD and the value of early proactive therapeutic drug monitoring with ustekinumab in paediatric Crohn's disease. Together with Dr. Aleixo Muise, and equipped with a $5M philanthropic donation to the SickKids IBD Centre, I am co-leading the implementation of a Precision IBD Program at SickKids that aims to integrate genomic and biomarker data into clinical decision-making.

2. Teaching Philosophy

My teaching philosophy, broadly, is to train the next generation of academic paediatric gastroenterologists, with a focus on IBD. Having had the privilege of being mentored by Drs. Griffiths and Kamath as a GI trainee, I recognize the paramount role that mentorship plays in shaping one's career; accordingly, mentorship is central to my own teaching philosophy. I wish to counsel and guide trainees and to help them achieve the scholarly and clinical knowledge, skills and experience needed to excel in academic positions across Canada and around the world, and to become leaders in their field. As a teacher, I strive to integrate the dissemination of theoretical knowledge in more formal settings, with teaching practical skills at the bedside and in clinic. I aim to create a learning environment that fosters critical thinking, problem-solving, and the ability to apply knowledge in real-world situations.
