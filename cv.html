C. Academic Profile
1. Research Statements
Liver Disease in IBD Patients with a Focus on PSC-IBD (2015–Present)

A major focus of my research program is liver disease in IBD patients, especially primary sclerosing cholangitis (PSC). PSC, though rare, represents a leading cause of morbidity and mortality in IBD populations, given the frequent need for liver transplant and the risk of colorectal and hepatobiliary malignancy. Whereas the study of PSC and IBD have traditionally been siloed, I have strived to redefine this paradigm and to study the two as a unit. During my PhD in Clinical Epidemiology, I compiled a SickKids PSC/PSC-IBD database spanning 20 years. I applied my expertise in longitudinal data analysis to define the gut phenotype of IBD observed in children with PSC. I also performed a prospective study demonstrating the frequent subclinical gut inflammation in PSC-IBD, which may contribute to the increased risk of colorectal dysplasia. I lend IBD and methodological expertise to several major international PSC consortia/networks, including the Pediatric PSC Consortium and the NIH-funded ChiLDReN PSC Prospective Cohort Study. In 2021, I secured a Crohn's and Colitis Canada grant ($375,000 CAD) to establish a multi-province Canadian PSC-IBD cohort linked to health administrative data. I am also a Co-Investigator on CIDsCaNN (second phase, $5M from the Ch.I.L.D. Foundation), an infrastructure I am leveraging to study PSC-IBD. I am one of 3 Co-PIs on a $2M grant to elucidate the single-cell landscape of the liver and gut in treatment-naïve paediatric PSC/PSC-IBD patients, and am Co-PI on a CIHR Team Microbiome grant focused on paediatric colitis patients, including those with PSC.

Precision Medicine in IBD (2018–Present)

Nowhere is the need for individualized treatment greater than in IBD, where there is a complete lack of data to inform positioning of therapies. While my initial research focus was largely clinical, to better tackle the topic of precision medicine in IBD, I have adopted a more translational approach. I am leading a multi-centre prospective study funded by the Crohn's and Colitis Foundation to examine proteomic and transcriptomic predictors of infliximab response in paediatric IBD. I am also PI on the SickKids GI-HEP Biobank, which has enrolled over 200 subjects (with a focus on IBD and PSC-IBD). The Biobank is closely tied to CIDsCaNN for detailed longitudinal clinical data collection. Equipped with these tools and a $5M philanthropic donation to the SickKids IBD Centre, I am co-leading, with Dr. Muise, an initiative to implement a Precision IBD Program at SickKids.

2. Teaching Philosophy

My teaching philosophy, broadly, is to train the next generation of academic paediatric gastroenterologists, with a focus on IBD. Having had the privilege of being mentored by Drs. Griffiths and Kamath as a GI trainee, I recognize the paramount role that mentorship plays in shaping one's career; accordingly, mentorship is central to my own teaching philosophy. I wish to counsel and guide trainees and to help them achieve the scholarly and clinical knowledge, skills and experience needed to excel in academic positions across Canada and around the world, and to become leaders in their field. As a teacher, I strive to integrate the dissemination of theoretical knowledge in more formal settings, with teaching practical skills at the bedside and in clinic. I aim to create a learning environment that fosters critical thinking, problem-solving, and the ability to apply knowledge in real-world situations.
